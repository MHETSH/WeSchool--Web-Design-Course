AL AHLY


Al-Ahly Gymnastics Club, or as it is known in short as Al-Ahly Club, is a professional Egyptian sports club that plays in the Egyptian Premier League, headquartered in Cairo, and it is the only club in Egypt besides Zamalek that has not been relegated to the second division

Al-Ahly Club was founded on April 24, 1907 by many dignitaries in Egypt, such as Omar Lotfi Bey, Amin Sami Pasha, Mustafa Kamel and Talaat Harb. It was established as a club for Egyptian patriotic youth, and since its inception, Egyptians gathered to spend their time and practice sports.


After that, the Al-Ahly club’s board of directors was established under the chairmanship of the Englishman Michael Innes, and in 1909 the first football stadium was established in the club’s history and was called at the time (Al-Hosh). And the number of games inside Al-Ahly Club in 1916 reached 4 games: football, tennis, billiards and gymnastics. Al-Ahly club has a strong competition with Zamalek, who are considered the two poles of Egyptian football, and their matches are known as the Cairo derby or the summit match. Al-Ahly Club is the most successful Egyptian, Arab and African club in the football game

Al-Ahly won 141 championships that placed it in the first place among the world’s clubs. At the local level, Al-Ahly club won the Egyptian Premier League Shield 42 times, the last of which was the Shield of 2020, and won the Egypt Cup 37 times, the last of which was in 2020, and also won the Egyptian Super Cup 11 times. In the Cairo region league 15 times, the Sultan Hussein Cup 7 times, he also won the United Arab Republic Cup once, and the Egyptian Federation Refresher Cup once. On the African level, the team won the African Champions League 10 times, the last of which was in 2021, during which it qualified for the Club World Cup for the seventh time in its history.

And the African Super Cup 7 times, in addition to the African Confederation Cup once, the African Cup for Cup Winners’ Clubs 4 times, the Afro-Asian Cup for Clubs once, and at the Arab level, the team won the Arab Cup for Champions Clubs once, the Arab Cup for Cup Winners Clubs once, and the Arab Cup Winners’ Cup once. The Arab Super twice, and Al-Ahly holds the record for winning the Egyptian Premier League, the Egypt Cup, the Egyptian Super Cup, the Cairo District League, the Sultan Hussein Cup, the United Arab Republic Cup, the Egyptian Federation Cup, the African Champions League, the African Super Cup, the African Club Cup, the Cup Winners’ Cup and the Arab Super Cup


Al-Ahly also holds the Egyptian record for winning the African Confederation Cup with Zamalek. Al-Ahly fans nicknamed the club several nicknames, namely, the Red Devils, the Red Genie, the Red Castle, and the National Club. Al-Ahly was chosen as the best African club in the twentieth century, according to the decision of the Confederation of African Football issued in 2000, and it was also chosen as the best club in the world according to the International Federation of Football History and Statistics.
